# Economics

## What Is a Converted Lead Worth to Credex?

The value of a converted lead depends on:
- average AI infrastructure spend
- Credex margin on discounted credits
- retention duration
- expansion potential

For this model, I assumed the average qualified customer:
- spends approximately $2,000/month on AI tooling or API usage
- saves 15–30% using discounted credits
- remains active for at least 12 months

If Credex captures even a modest margin of:
- 8–12%

on redirected AI infrastructure spend, then a single converted customer could reasonably generate:

```txt
$150–$400/month gross profit
```

Assuming:
- average gross profit = $250/month
- average retention = 12 months

Estimated customer value:

```txt
$3,000 LTV
```

This is likely conservative for larger engineering teams with meaningful API spend.

# What Conversion Rates Make This Profitable?

Assume:
- 100 completed audits
- 20% become qualified leads
- 25% of qualified leads book consultations
- 30% of consultations convert to purchases

That results in:

```txt
100 audits
→ 20 qualified leads
→ 5 consultations
→ 1.5 paying customers
```

Using estimated LTV:

```txt
1.5 × $3,000 = $4,500 value
```

Even if acquisition costs for those 100 audits reached:
```txt
$500–$1000
```

the funnel would still likely remain profitable.

# What Would Need to Be True to Reach $1M ARR in 18 Months?

Assume average annual revenue contribution per customer:

```txt
$3,000 ARR
```

To reach:

```txt
$1,000,000 ARR
```

Credex would need approximately:

```txt
333 active customers
```

If:
- 3% of completed audits convert into paying customers

then required audit volume becomes:

```txt
333 / 0.03
≈ 11,100 completed audits
```

Over 18 months:

```txt
~617 audits/month
~20 audits/day
```

That is realistically achievable if:
- the audit tool becomes shareable
- SEO content compounds
- AI tooling discussions remain highly active
- Credex leverages partnerships and founder communities

# Why the Model Could Work

The strongest aspect of the economics is that:
- the product is free
- user pain is immediate and measurable
- savings are quantifiable
- the tool naturally creates high-intent leads

Most SaaS lead generation tools struggle because value is abstract.

This product is different because:
- users immediately see a dollar amount
- optimization recommendations feel actionable
- financial waste creates urgency

The combination of:
- utility
- shareability
- measurable ROI

makes the acquisition model potentially efficient despite operating in a crowded AI tooling market.

---

# Biggest Risk

The biggest economic risk is that AI vendor pricing changes rapidly.

If:
- vendors aggressively reduce prices
- bundled subscriptions improve
- users consolidate around fewer providers

then optimization opportunities may shrink over time.

To remain valuable long-term, the product would likely need to evolve from:
- simple plan optimization

into:
- benchmarking
- workflow analytics
- API usage analysis
- procurement optimization
- organizational AI governance
